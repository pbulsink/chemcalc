This is due to the natual distribution of isotopes that composes each element. Chlorine has isotopes at approximately 35 and 37 amu. Similarly, carbon has isotopes at 12 and 13 amu, and hydrogen has isotopes at 1 and 2 amu. Element masses on the periodic table are a weighted average of all the isotopes (for chlorine, it's 35.453). However, for mass spec and other methods that look at individual molecule masses, the exact mass of the isotopes composing that molecule is important. The distribution comes from having one or more atoms of higher isotope mass be present in a molecule.
