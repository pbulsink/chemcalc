Instructions:

Enter your formula in the proper box. Formulas can be linear (e.g. CH3CH(CH3)CH2CH2OH) or elemental (e.g. C5H12O), and can contain solvents or salts of crystallization (eg AuCl3*H2O).

To generate a theoretical elemental analysis, just press calculate.

Solvent inclusion calculations to correct experimental results can be performed by entering experimental data and choosing possible solvent interferences.

For best and quickest result use only possible interferences, don't select all solvents. More than 3 solvents likely won't finish calculating before the program is killed.

Please note that this is still under development, and may contain errors.

This website holds no guarantee that calculated values are correct, and will not be responsible if incorrect values poorly influence future experimentation or have an effect on publishing papers.
